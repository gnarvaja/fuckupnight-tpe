Scalability & Profit

From a founder perspective

We are used to think scalability and profit from an Investor perspective. We tell to our potential investors: "our valuation is 1M, but we will scale across many countries, so your investment will be worth 10x or 100x". And "no, this business is not just a way of living for me and a couple of guys, we are going to make big profits".

But from your perspective as founder, they also matter.

If I go at the beginning, one of the main reasons for starting my own company was freedom, to choose the technologies I wanted to use, to take my own decisions and risks. But as your business grows you gradually lose that freedom, you now have responsabilities with your customers, with your team.

When you start is natural and the best thing for you to do many different tasks, to learn and because you don't have a big team. But after months or years, you will become tired of doing many of those tasks and the best thing is to delegate them to new and speciallized team members. To be able to do that, you need to scale, if you don't, you will be doing that ugly 4-hours a week task your entire life.

And profits, well, first if all, if you want at some point leave the company, to exit, it's easier to do it if your company has profits. But also, profits are important to be able to recreate your company or parts of it when needed. If you don't have enought profits, you will be seeing every day those mistakes you made in the first years, without having the resources to fix them.

Happy ending
